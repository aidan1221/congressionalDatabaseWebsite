CongressDBQ

About The Project:

This project began almost 6 months ago as a Graduate level databases project with the goal to simply create a PostgreSQL database from congressional data scraped from Congress.gov. The motivation to expand this idea into what you see now was driven by the realization that the information contained within the walls of Congress.gov is fairly inaccessible. Obfuscated by layers of government complexity, site redirection, and iffy search filters, it can often take several minutes to find the information you are looking for within the site, and when you do, it is difficult to find patterns or meaning in what you are looking at.

The goal of this site is to facilitate a user flow that allows for easy access to the congressional information you are seeking, as well as examples of data analysis and visualizations created by querying the underlying database. If you are interested in full access to the data or database, contact Aidan Wolfe.
